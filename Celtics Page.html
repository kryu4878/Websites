Boston Celtics - The Greatest Organization

The History of the Boston Celtics

The organization was founded in 1946, and the Celtics are regarded as one of the most successful basketball teams in NBA history. The franchise is tied for the most NBA Championships won with 17.

Many legendary players have played for the Boston Celtics, such as Larry Bird, Bill Russel, Ray Allen, Kevin Garnett, and Paul Pierce. The last NBA Championship that the Celtics have won was in 2008. But since then, the team has made multiple NBA Finals appearances as well as Eastern Conference Finals.

What the Future Holds

Although the 2020-2021 season wasn’t great for the Celtics, filled with COVID protocols and injuries, they can still look forward to the next season. As soon as the season ended for the Celtics, they quickly adjusted the team by bringing in a new Head Coach, Ime Udoka. With the acquisition of Udoka, they hope to create a different locker room atmosphere and look to have a new identity in the upcoming season.

New Additions + Losses

The Celtics needed to make roster changes for the upcoming season. To create roster spots, they traded and have waived Carsen Edwards as well as Tremont Waters. Now, only two of the four rookies drafted in 2020 remains on the team: Romeo Langford and Grant Williams. While getting rid of the two players, the Celtics quickly filled them up by signing Josh Richardson and Enes Kanter, as well as their latest addition, Dennis Schroder. It’ll be fun to see what these new guys will bring to the team.

Key Moves

The Celtics were able to resign both Marcus Smart and Robert Williams III to an extension. Smart is the heart of the Celtics team and one of the best defenders in the league. Also with Williams III having a fantastic year last season, the Celtics can expect big things from these two players.

Fire and Ice

Whether or not the Celtics do well next season depends on Jayson Tatum and Jaylen Brown. They’re both superstars in the making, and depending on how they perform next season will decide how well the season goes for the Celtics. Both, Tatum and Brown, have improved every season, so Celtics fans can expect the same from their young stars.

Bradley Beal

The reason why the Boston Celtics didn’t make big moves this offseason is so that they could leave cap space to trade for Bradley Beal. If Celtics can acquire Beal from the Wizards before the trade deadline, then they would definitely be one of the top contenders in the Eastern Conference.

Points of Emphasis:

    • Stay healthy
    • More team basketball
    • Get to the free throw line
    • Get Bradley Beal
    • Make playoffs
